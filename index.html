Talk track
Objection Handling
Fast responses to common candidate hesitations.
"My employer has always looked after me."
→ "Has that care been proactive — or reactive when you've pushed?"
"The counteroffer is more than I expected."
→ "Why wasn't this available before you started looking?"
"I don't want to let my team down."
→ "Is your career being shaped by what's right for you — or by discomfort?"
Principle: acknowledge first, then redirect.

Discovery
Motivation Patterns
Separate deep motivations from surface ones.
Deep (survive salary match): leadership ceiling, autonomy, culture, career scope, recognition
Surface (resolved by counteroffer): base salary, commute, title-only uplift, temporary frustration
"Which reasons would still matter if salary was matched?"
"If money were identical, which role would you choose?"
Goal: anchor decisions on structural, not financial, reasons.
◷
Workflow
Stage-Based Guidance
What to focus on at each point in the process.
Initial: Establish the real reason for moving. "Not the job — the feeling."
Interview: Reconnect to motivation. "Which parts are about 2 years, not 2 weeks?"
Offer: Test the counteroffer scenario. "What would it need to include to genuinely change your thinking?"
Resignation: Rehearse the conversation. Keep it short and factual.
Rule: what you do at each stage shapes what happens at the next.
⬡
Risk
Counteroffer Patterns
Prepare candidates for the four most common types.
Salary-only match — Most common. Does not resolve progression or scope.
Promotion + salary — More persuasive. Has the day-to-day actually changed?
Emotional appeal from manager — Separate loyalty from logic.
Delayed promise — Why wasn't this offered already?
Prep candidates for the specific type they're most likely to face.
★
Commercial
Client Selling Points
Sell the opportunity properly — not just the salary.
Growth story: where is this company heading in 2–3 years?
Manager quality: what makes this manager better than their current one?
Career runway: what does the next promotion look like here?
Total package: equity, pension, flexibility — not just base salary.
Culture proof: specific examples, not generic phrases.
If you can't answer these, your candidate can't defend the move.
